The Complete AI Guide for Beginners in 2026: Understand Everything in 15 Minutes

You hear about AI everywhere but don't know where to start? This guide gives you the essential basics in 15 minutes of reading.

Open this guide on the homepage (expandable with other guides)

Welcome to the age of artificial intelligence. If you're reading this article, it's probably because you hear about AI everywhere and don't know how to navigate it yet. This guide will explain the essentials, without jargon, in 15 minutes. What is AI in simple terms? Artificial intelligence is a set of technologies that allow computers to perform tasks that previously required human intelligence: understanding language, recognizing images, making decisions, creating content. What we call 'generative AI' (ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney) is the technology that generates new content — text, images, audio, video — from a simple description

How it works (simplified)

these models were trained on billions of documents, books, websites and code. They learned the patterns of human language and can predict the most likely continuation of a text. It's not magic — it's very sophisticated statistics

The main models to know in 2026

ChatGPT (OpenAI) — the best known, GPT-4o in limited free version, GPT-4o and o3 in paid version. Claude (Anthropic) — known for thoughtful responses, ethical reasoning and the ability to handle long documents. Gemini (Google) — integrated into Google services, strong on web search. Mistral — the European champion, open-source, fast. Llama (Meta) — the most powerful open-source model

How to start

create a free account on Claude.ai or ChatGPT.com

Start with simple tasks

ask to explain a concept, summarize a text, correct an email. Progress to more advanced uses as you understand the capabilities

What AI CANNOT do

access the internet in real time (except specific versions), remember your previous conversations (except with memory feature enabled), have real opinions or emotions, or guarantee the accuracy of all its answers. AI is here to stay and will continue to evolve rapidly — better to get comfortable with this tool now
